PROJECTS

ColorMatch for Android

Programmer

Fall 2014-Winter 2015

Mission

The purpose of this project was to get familiarized with the Android-development process and to strengthen our object-oriented programming skills.

Idea

Our idea was to design a videogame for Android- powered devices, where we used Canvas for our bitmap drawings and graphic handling and SQLite for our highscore database management.

Results

ColorMatch for Android was published on May 27, 2015 without major issues. However, prior to publishing, somebody forked our GitHub repository and published an early version of our app. Luckily, the fake-version was then deleted from the Google Play Store.

Quadcopter

Hardware designer and programmer

Fall 2015

Mission

The purpose of this project was to team up with the Carleton University Robotics Club to build a flying quadcopter that achieve steady flying.

Idea

Our idea was to use a Multiwii microcontroller, an Arduino-based flight controller, to control the four motors according to the readings given by the accelerometer, barometer and magnetometer.

Results

We completed the quadcopter design on November 25, 2015. Using the Stable-mode and Barometer-hold of the Multiwii we achieved stable flying. The Carleton Univerisity Robotics Club showed our design at the Ottawa Maker Faire 2015.

Team

Zack Munich, Eric Lanteigne and Erick Kad-Bay.

CUDrone

Hardware designer and programmer

Winter 2015

Mission

The purpose of this project is to team with Stay Late And Make (SLAM), to build a quadcopter capable of autonomous flying.

Idea

Our idea for this project is to design a drone to work along several sonars for collision-avoidance and GPS-hold and Magnetometer-hold flight modes for a point A to point B autonomous flying.

Results

The project's funding was approved by the University committee. We are currently buying parts to begin the first stage of building the quadcopter.
